**Slide 1 – Introduction**

Hello everyone. Today I want to tell you about Geoffrey Hinton, one of the pioneers of artificial intelligence. We will explore his life and see why his work matters to all of us.

**Slide 2 – Background & Early Life**

Hinton was born in London in 1947. He studied psychology because he wanted to know how the human brain learns. This curiosity led him to build simple computer models inspired by our brains, called neural networks. Many people didn’t believe in these ideas. When research money in the United States came mainly from the military, he moved to Canada so he could work without such ties.

**Slide 3 – Skills & Obstacles**

Hinton invented important tools for AI. In the early 1980s he made the Boltzmann machine, which helps computers find patterns in data. A few years later he helped develop a training method called backpropagation, which lets a network learn by adjusting its connections. These techniques are still used in most AI systems. He did this work at a time when few supported him, but he kept going because he believed in his approach.

**Slide 4 – Career Highlights**

His work changed the field. After his early inventions, his students built a program that could recognise pictures much better than before, changing how scientists and companies viewed AI. He later helped start research labs and received major science prizes for his contributions. In 2023 he left his job at Google so he could speak openly about the risks of AI.

**Slide 5 – Impact & Inspiration**

Hinton’s ideas are part of everyday life. Face recognition on phones, voice assistants and translation apps use methods he pioneered. He encourages young scientists to mix ideas from different areas, be patient and think about the effects of their work.

**Slide 6 – Conclusion**

To finish: Geoffrey Hinton shows that curiosity and persistence can change the world. He laid the foundations for deep learning and reminds us to build AI responsibly. I hope his story inspires you to learn more about this exciting field. Thank you!
